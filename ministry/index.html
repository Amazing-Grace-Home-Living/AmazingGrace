5.2 The Sequenced Roadmap for Incorporation and Exemption

The establishment of a Nonprofit Religious Corporation in California requires a precise, sequenced legal approach to avoid bureaucratic delays and unnecessary fees.

  • Phase 1: Articles of Incorporation (State Level) - The entity must first file the Articles of Incorporation of a Nonprofit Religious Corporation (Form ARTS-RE) with the California Secretary of State. This foundational document legally establishes the entity within the state. The articles must include the organization's exact name, its specific religious purpose, the designated agent for service of process, and appropriate dedication and dissolution clauses. Financial Commitment: A standard $30 filing fee applies.
  • Phase 2: Statement of Information - Within exactly 90 days of filing the initial Articles of Incorporation, the new entity must file a Statement of Information (Form SI-100) with the Secretary of State. This form legally updates the state on the organization's current physical address, officers, and board of directors. Financial Commitment: A $20 filing fee is required.
  • Phase 3: Federal 501(c)(3) Exemption (IRS) - To legally receive tax-deductible donations and apply for institutional grants, the entity must apply for federal tax exemption. For smaller organizations projecting less than $50,000 in annual gross receipts, the streamlined Form 1023-EZ is highly recommended. Financial Commitment: The user fee for Form 1023-EZ is $275.
  • Phase 4: California State Tax Exemption and Registry of Charitable Trusts - The organization must submit Form 3500A (Submission of Exemption Request) to the Franchise Tax Board once the IRS federal determination letter is received to avoid the CA minimum annual franchise tax. The organization must also submit the Initial Registration Form (Form CT-1) to the Attorney General’s Registry of Charitable Trusts within 30 days of initially receiving its first assets. Financial Commitment: $50 registration fee for CT-1.
